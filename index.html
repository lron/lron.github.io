From January 2016 I have moved to the realm of pedagogics and I'm a lecturer (adjunkt, in Danish) at KEA - Copenhagen School of Design and Technology (Københavns Erhvervsakademi, in Danish), in the DIGITAL program.

From January 2013 to December 2015 I was a postdoc at the DemTech research project, led by Carsten Schürmann, at the IT University of Copenhagen, in Denmark. My research here was focused on cryptography applied to e-voting, which ranges from homomorphic encryption, to all those mechanisms and cryptographic primitives that form the basis of a secure, robust, private and E2E verifiable voting protocol.

Previously, I was a PhD student at the Combinatorics, Coding and Security Group (CCSG) from the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. My PhD thesis was focused on coding theory and data-hiding, entitled "On additive binary nonlinear codes and steganography".

During my PhD I also did a 4-months research stay in collaboration with Gary McGuire. at the Claude Shannon Institute, University College Dublin, in Ireland.

If you would like to have a copy of my CV, please, contact me.

Research interests

My research interests are on electronic voting (everlasting privacy, secret sharing, coercion-freeness), cryptography (zero-knowledge proofs, commitment schemes, homomorphic encryption, APN functions), data-hiding (steganography, copyright protection), and coding theory (Z4-linear codes, nonlinear codes, perfect codes, syndrome coding problem).

PhD thesis

I did my PhD at the Combinatorics, Coding and Security Group (CCSG) in the department of Information and Communications Engineering (dEIC), at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona, in Spain.

My supervisor was Josep Rifà, head of the research group.

My PhD was focused on applying coding theory to the branch of data-hiding known as steganography. Further information about the defense, abstract and full text can be found here.

Consulting tasks

I have been a member of the evaluation committee of the vVote electronic voting system, which was developed by the Victorial electoral commmission, Australia. Our security audit included an analysis of the cryptographic components (in particular, the mix-net) of the voting system, a code review and static code analysis. Our report can be accessed here.
