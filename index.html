About me

From January 2013 to December 2015 I have been a postdoct at the DemTech research project, led by Carsten Schürmann, at the IT University of Copenhagen, in Denmark. My research here is focused on cryptography applied to e-voting, which ranges from homomorphic encryption, to all those mechanisms and cryptographic primitives that form the basis of a secure, robust, private and E2E verifiable voting protocol.

Previously, I was a PhD student at the Combinatorics, Coding and Security Group (CCSG) from the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona. My PhD thesis was focused on coding theory and data-hiding, entitled "On additive binary nonlinear codes and steganography". My PhD was supported by the Agency for Administration of University and Research Grants (AGAUR) of the Catalan government.

During my PhD I also did a 4-months research stay at the Claude Shannon Institute from the University College Dublin, in Ireland. There I mostly focused on the study of Almost Perfect Nonlinear (APN) functions over abelian groups of order 16, in collaboration with Gary McGuire.

Research interests

My research interests are on electronic voting (everlasting privacy, secret sharing, coercion-freeness), cryptography (zero-knowledge proofs, commitment schemes, homomorphic encryption, APN functions), data-hiding (steganography, copyright protection), and coding theory (Z4-linear codes, nonlinear codes, perfect codes, syndrome coding problem).

PhD thesis

I did my PhD at the Combinatorics, Coding and Security Group (CCSG) in the department of Information and Communications Engineering (dEIC), at the Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona, in Spain.

My supervisor was Josep Rifà, head of the research group.

My PhD was focused on applying coding theory to the branch of data-hiding known as steganography. Further information about the defense, abstract and full text can be found here.
